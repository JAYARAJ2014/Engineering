# Engineering Manager Responsibilities

Engineering Managers operate across three core pillars:

1. **People Leadership**  
2. **Delivery & Execution**  
3. **Technical Direction & Quality**  


---

## 1. People Leadership

### 1.1 Build, grow, and mentor the team
- Conduct regular 1:1s  
- Coach engineers on soft and hard skills  
- Create growth and development plans  
- Identify skill gaps and training needs  

### 1.2 Hiring and team scaling
- Write job descriptions  
- Conduct interviews  
- Build a diverse and balanced team  
- Onboard new engineers effectively  

### 1.3 Performance management
- Set clear expectations  
- Identify and address performance issues early  
- Provide constructive feedback  
- Manage promotions and PIPs when required  

### 1.4 Foster psychological safety & team health
- Encourage open communication  
- Remove toxic behavior  
- Promote trust and collaboration  
- Facilitate conflict resolution  


---

## 2. Delivery & Execution

### 2.1 Own project delivery
- Ensure engineering commitments are met  
- Break large projects into deliverable chunks  
- Track progress and remove blockers  
- Partner with PMs for roadmap clarity  

### 2.2 Improve execution discipline
- Enforce code reviews, testing, and quality gates  
- Ensure engineering standards are followed  
- Oversee standups, sprint planning, and retros  

### 2.3 Prioritize work
- Balance business priorities against engineering needs  
- Make trade-offs visible  
- Say “no” when needed to protect the team  

### 2.4 Risk management
- Identify risks early  
- Handle escalations  
- Communicate proactively with stakeholders  


---

## 3. Technical Direction & Quality

### 3.1 Ensure good engineering practices
- Code quality and architecture principles  
- Testing strategy  
- CI/CD adoption  
- Documentation standards  

### 3.2 Provide technical guidance
- Review designs  
- Unblock engineers  
- Ensure scalable, maintainable solutions  
- Encourage correct design patterns  

### 3.3 Drive long-term technical excellence
- Operational maturity (monitoring, logging, SLAs, SLOs)  
- Reduce technical debt  
- Lead migrations and refactors  
- Embed security best practices  


---

## 4. Stakeholder Management

### 4.1 Partner with Product Management
- Align engineering execution with product goals  
- Balance new features vs tech debt vs stability  
- Provide accurate estimates  

### 4.2 Work cross-functionally
- Collaborate with QA, SRE, Security, Support, Design  
- Ensure alignment across teams  

### 4.3 Communication
- Provide clear status updates  
- Communicate risks early  
- Translate engineering work into business impact  


---

## 5. Operational Excellence

### 5.1 Own the on-call & incident process
- Manage on-call rotations  
- Reduce MTTR  
- Run blameless postmortems  

### 5.2 Improve availability and performance
- Strengthen monitoring and alerting  
- Define SLOs, SLIs, SLAs  
- Ensure proper capacity planning  

### 5.3 Cost efficiency
- Cloud cost awareness  
- Optimize infrastructure usage  


---

## 6. Culture & Leadership

### 6.1 Model the right behaviors
- Ownership  
- Transparency  
- Accountability  
- Collaboration  

### 6.2 Build an inclusive culture
- Encourage diverse viewpoints  
- Reduce single points of failure  
- Give equal voice in discussions  

### 6.3 Create a high-performance environment
- Align personal and team goals  
- Protect team from distractions  
- Celebrate wins and recognize contributions  


---

## Summary

An Engineering Manager is responsible for:

- Growing and mentoring engineers  
- Ensuring timely, high-quality delivery  
- Improving engineering discipline  
- Guiding architecture and technical excellence  
- Managing performance and team health  
- Collaborating with product and business partners  
- Running operations and incident processes  
- Building a strong, inclusive, high-performing culture
