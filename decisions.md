### **Step 1: Define the Decision Clearly**
Ask:
- What decision am I trying to make?
- What problem am I solving?
- What does success look like?

### **Step 2: Identify the Minimum Data Needed**
Avoid data overload.  
Ask:
- What *minimum* data points directly influence this decision?
- What do we already have?
- What is missing?

### **Step 3: Pull & Analyze the Data**
Look for:
- Trends (Is it going up or down?)
- Comparisons (Before vs after, A vs B)
- Outliers (Whatâ€™s unusual?)
- Patterns (What repeats?)

### **Step 4: Make the Decision**
Use:
- 60â€“70% clarity rule  
- Impact vs effort  
- Risk vs reward  
- Reversible vs irreversible decisions  

**Act â†’ measure â†’ adjust.**

### **Step 5: Measure the Outcome**
After rollout:
- Did metrics move in the expected direction?
- What surprised us?
- What should we adjust?

This creates a learning loop.
